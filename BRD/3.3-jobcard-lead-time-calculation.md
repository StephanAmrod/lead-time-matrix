# 3.3. Jobcard Lead Time Calculation

Each jobcard must result in:
- **Internal Lead Time**
- **Client Lead Time**

This section describes the calculations for determining the different lead times for a jobcard, as well as a visual representation.

## 1. Determine Matrix Lead Time

The system must retrieve the lead time from the Production Lead Time Matrix based on:

- **Branding Department**
- **Branding Group**
- **Quantity Break**

This value is referred to as **Matrix Lead Time**.

### Additional Notes

- Production-related lead time adjustments occur during a separate downstream process.
- This will be addressed during the NPR stage/process.
- It should not be confused with the current Lead Time Matrix functionality.

---

## 2. Apply Overrides and Adjustments

The system must determine whether an override or adjustment exists at:

- **Branding Department level**
- **Branding Group level**

### Rules

1. **If an override exists:**
   - Base Lead Time = Override
   - Overrides must take precedence over all other rules

2. **If no override exists and an adjustment exists:**
   - Base Lead Time = Matrix Lead Time + Adjustment

3. **If neither exists:**
   - Base Lead Time = Matrix Lead Time

### Result: Base Lead Time

### Impact on Existing Orders

To clarify lead time override impact on existing orders:

- **If an order has already been approved and paid, and a lead time override/update is made on the matrix:**
  - The order's lead time must not be pushed out/extended
  - The existing scheduled timeline should remain unchanged

- **Only orders that are:**
  - Not yet approved
  - Not yet paid
  - Not ready for print/production

  should be impacted by lead time changes.

---

> **Note: Orders Already Scheduled for Production**
>
> Orders that are already approved, paid, and scheduled should be treated as locked. Lead time matrix changes should only apply to future/unprocessed orders.

---

## 3. Determine Add-On Lead Times

The system must identify all applicable add-on components for the jobcard.

### Supported Components

- **Console Warehouse** - Lead Time Matrix
- **CMT** - Production Lead Time Matrix
- **Personalisation** - Production Lead Time Matrix

### Calculation Requirements

Each component must:

- Be calculated independently
- Use the relevant matrix and rules

Be based on:

- **Branding Department**
- **Group**
- **Quantity Break**

### Result: Add-Ons Lead Time

---

## 4. Calculate Total Lead Time (Internal)

The system must calculate the **Total Lead Time** (Internal).

### Formula

```
Total Lead Time = Base Lead Time + Sum of all Add-On Lead Times
```

### Rules

- Multiple add-ons must be summed cumulatively
- Each add-on may only be applied once
- If no add-ons apply:
  - Total Lead Time = Base Lead Time

---

## 5. Calculate Client Lead Time

The system must calculate the **Client Lead Time**.

### Rules

- **If Console is NOT present:**
  - Client Lead Time = Total Lead Time

- **If Console IS present:**
  - Client Lead Time = Total Lead Time + 1 day

---

## 6. Due Date Calculation (Jobcard)

The system must calculate:

- **Internal Due Date**
- **Client Due Date**

### Rules

- **Internal Due Date** = Approval/Payment Date + Total Lead Time

- **Client Due Date** = Approval/Payment Date + Client Lead Time
