# 3.8. Exceptions

The system must support configurable exceptions to due date and lead time rules.

---

## 1. Logo24 Orders

Logo24 orders must be treated as a special case.

### Requirements

- The system must **identify Logo24 orders** based on configuration
- **Logo24 rules must be configurable** and may:
  - Override standard lead time rules
  - Apply different due date logic
  - Modify working day behaviour

### Rules

**Any Logo24 order paid and approved before 17:00 will be ready the next day. If the order is approved and paid after 17:00, it will be ready the day after.**

#### 1. Orders Placed Monday to Thursday

The LOGO24 service operates from **Monday to Thursday on trading days in South Africa**. All clients must place their orders and cash-on-delivery clients must make their payments by **5 PM**. Orders submitted by this deadline will be ready for collection in Johannesburg by **5 PM the following day**, provided it is a trading day in South Africa.

#### 2. Orders Placed on Friday

Orders that are placed and paid for before **12 PM on a Friday** (if it is a trading day in South Africa) will be completed by **5 PM the following Monday** in Johannesburg, provided that Monday is also a trading day. Orders placed after 12 PM on a Friday, as well as those made during weekends or on South African public holidays, will **not qualify for the LOGO24 service** and will be treated as standard orders, subject to regular branding lead times.

---

## 2. Branch Orders

Lead times for branch deliveries may be defined in one of two ways:
- **As a number of days**, or
- **As specific delivery days of the week**

The system must determine the final due date based on the configured method.

### 1. Lead Time Defined as Number of Days

- The branch lead time must be treated as an **additive component**.
- The system must **add the branch lead time (in days)** to the calculated total lead time.
- The final due date must then be calculated using **standard working day rules**.

### 2. Lead Time Defined as Specific Days of the Week

- The branch lead time must be treated as a **delivery constraint, not an additive value**.

#### Calculation Logic

The system must:

1. **First calculate the base due date:** Approval/Payment Date + Total Lead Time
2. **Then adjust the due date** to the next available valid delivery day for the branch

#### Rules

- If the calculated due date **falls on a valid delivery day**, **no adjustment is required**.
- If the calculated due date **does not fall on a valid delivery day**, the system must **move the due date to the next specified delivery day**.
